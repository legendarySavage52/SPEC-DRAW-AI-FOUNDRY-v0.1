# The Legendary Savage Manifesto

## Why Spec-Draw Exists

I started Spec-Draw because I watched manufacturing companies treat their most critical processes like they were secrets locked in filing cabinets.

Drawings. Specifications. Standard Operating Procedures. Quality requirements. Safety standards.

All scattered.

All hidden.

All vulnerable to being lost if one person left the company.

**Spec-Draw was built to change that.**

## The Problem We Solve

### Before Spec-Draw

- Drawings are scattered across multiple systems
- Specifications are buried in emails and spreadsheets
- Version control is manual and error-prone
- Knowledge walks out the door when experienced people leave
- Training new employees takes months
- Quality incidents happen because information wasn't accessible
- Compliance audits are nightmares

### After Spec-Draw

- One central source of truth for all technical documentation
- Instant access to the latest specifications and drawings
- Complete version history and audit trails
- Knowledge is preserved and transferable
- New employees get up to speed in weeks, not months
- Quality and safety are built into the documentation process
- Compliance audits become straightforward

## The Values We Won't Compromise

### 1. Safety First

Manufacturing is where safety matters. Not as an afterthought. Not as compliance theater. As the first principle.

Every line of code we write, every feature we build, must make manufacturing safer.

### 2. Integrity Over Growth

We will never:
- Sell data that isn't ours to sell
- Cut corners on security to ship faster
- Hide problems from customers
- Build features that don't serve the user

If integrity and growth conflict, integrity wins. Every time.

### 3. Respect for Domain Expertise

We're building software for engineers, quality professionals, and manufacturing leaders who spent decades in this industry.

We listen more than we talk.
We ask before we assume.
We respect that they know their work better than we ever will.

### 4. Knowledge is Power, Information is Duty

We believe information should be:
- Accessible (not locked behind paywalls or complexity)
- Understandable (not jargon-filled)
- Actionable (not theoretical)
- Preservable (not lost when someone leaves)

### 5. Build to Last

We're not chasing trends.
We're not pivoting every quarter.
We're building infrastructure that will be valuable in 10 years, not just this quarter.

Every commit leaves the codebase better than it found it.
Every feature is documented.
Every decision is explained.

## What We're Building

Not just an application.

An ecosystem that allows manufacturing companies to:

- Document their knowledge
- Preserve their expertise
- Train their teams
- Ensure quality
- Maintain compliance
- Scale without losing what makes them great

## The Long Game

Picture this three years from now.

A manufacturing company uses Spec-Draw.

A senior engineer retires after 35 years with the company.

The company doesn't panic. The knowledge doesn't leave. It's all there in Spec-Draw.

A new engineer is hired. They go through the training. They access the specifications. They understand not just what to do, but why it matters.

Quality goes up. Safety incidents go down. Efficiency improves.

That's the dream.

## The Legacy

I measure Spec-Draw's success not by how much revenue it generates, but by how many manufacturing companies are safer, more efficient, and more knowledgeable because it exists.

If Spec-Draw helps preserve knowledge, improve safety, and make manufacturing work better for people who do that work, then we've succeeded.

If it makes me some money along the way, that's a bonus.

But the mission comes first.
